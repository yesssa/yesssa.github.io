R&B's Genre

R&B is a genre of popular music that combines jazz, gospel and blues,
this type of flow was first introduced by African-American musicians. The term was first used as
a marketing term in music in the United States in 1947 by Jerry Wexler who worked for Billboard magazine.

Early R&B

Early R&B

Early on the term "rhythm and blues" was used for boogie woogie.
American swing, jazz, and blues. All of these styles influenced the development
what is called rhythm and blues today.

Motown and Soul

Motown and Soul

AllMusic cites Motown as the pioneering
label of pop-soul, a style of soul music with raw vocals,
but polished production and toned-down subject
matter intended for pop radio and crossover success.

Funk and Disco

Funk and Disco

Funk music is a style of dance music that evolved from the Black R&B,
soul, and jazz scenes in the mid-1960s. Funk music is characterized by funky, syncopated
bass lines and steady, infectious drum grooves, which drove it to become one of
the most popular genres in the 1970s and '80s

Dance and Rap

Dance and Rap

Contemporary R&B (or simply R&B) is a popular music genre that combines
rhythm and blues with elements of pop, soul, funk, hip hop, and electronic music. The genre
features a distinctive record production style, drum machine-backed rhythms, pitch corrected
vocals and a smooth, lush style of vocal arrangement.
